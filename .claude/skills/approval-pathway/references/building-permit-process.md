# Ontario Building Permit Process

## When a Building Permit Is Required

A building permit is legally required for:
- Constructing new buildings **larger than 10 m²**
- Building additions
- Material or structural alterations (removing load-bearing walls, changing room sizes)
- Installing or modifying plumbing and HVAC systems
- Adding a **secondary suite**
- Finishing basements involving structural or plumbing changes
- Changing a building's use
- Building decks **more than 60 cm above grade**

**No permit generally required for:**
- Cosmetic work (painting, flooring, cabinetry)
- Replacing plumbing fixtures in the exact same location
- Replacing same-sized windows or doors without structural changes
- Storage sheds under **15 m²** with no plumbing

## Application Steps

### Step 1: Pre-Planning and Clearances
- Confirm compliance with the local Zoning By-law (setbacks, height, lot coverage)
- Secure all "Applicable Law" pre-approvals **before** submitting the building permit application:
  - Conservation Authority permits (if in TRCA regulated area)
  - Heritage Alteration Permits (if designated heritage property)
  - Site Plan Approvals (if site plan control applies)
  - Committee of Adjustment approval (if minor variance required)

### Step 2: Prepare Drawings
Plans must be prepared by a qualified designer with a **Building Code Identification Number (BCIN)**, a licensed architect, or a professional engineer.

### Step 3: Submission and Payment
Submit complete application and supporting documents — often via a municipal online portal (e.g., ePlans) — and pay required permit fees.

### Step 4: Municipal Review
The municipality conducts:
1. **Pre-screen check** (completeness)
2. **Detailed technical review** by departments: Zoning, Building Code, Mechanical/Plumbing, Fire

### Step 5: Revisions
If examiners find issues, a **deficiency notice** is issued. Revised drawings must be submitted. The statutory clock **stops** until revisions are received.

### Step 6: Permit Issuance
Once plans comply with all codes, the permit is issued and must be **visibly posted on site**.

## Required Documents / Drawings

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Application for a Permit to Construct or Demolish | Schedule 1: Designer Information |
| Property Survey and Site Plan | Shows lot lines, setbacks, existing/proposed structures |
| Lot Grading Plan | Required for new home construction |
| Architectural Drawings | Floor plans, exterior elevations, cross-sections, roof plans |
| Structural and Mechanical Drawings | Load-bearing elements, beam sizes, plumbing layouts, HVAC |
| Energy Efficiency Design Summary | SB-12 forms |
| Applicable Law Declarations | e.g., Tree Declarations |

## Inspection Stages

Permit holder must schedule municipal inspections at mandatory milestones **before** work is covered up.

| Stage | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| Footings and Foundations | Before pouring concrete; prior to backfilling |
| Structural Framing | Before installing insulation |
| Plumbing Rough-in | Before closing walls |
| HVAC Rough-in | Before closing walls |
| Insulation and Vapour Barrier | Before installing drywall |
| Fire Separations | Before covering with drywall |
| Final Inspection / Occupancy | Upon completion |

**Note**: Electrical inspections are handled separately by the **Electrical Safety Authority (ESA)**, not the municipality.

---

## Statutory Timelines for Permit Issuance

Once a **complete** application is submitted, the Ontario Building Code requires:

| Building Type | Response Deadline |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Standard residential house | **10 business days** |
| Complex projects (e.g., hospital) | **30 days** |

The clock stops if the application has deficiencies until revisions are submitted.

## 2024 and 2026 OBC Updates

### Secondary Suites (2024)
- Definition of "house" removed — homes with up to **two suites** receive code exemptions
- Lower ceiling heights permitted: **1,850 mm over stairs** in secondary suites
- Lower door heights: **1,890 mm** in secondary suites
- **Smoke-tight barriers** using 5/8" Type X drywall permitted instead of full fire-resistance ratings between units
- All smoke/CO alarms in a two-unit house must be **interconnected**

### Radon Mitigation (2024 — harmonized with National Building Code)
- All new Ontario homes require:
  - **Radon rough-in**: sub-floor depressurization pipe
  - Floor slab with continuous **air and damp-proofing barrier** to mitigate soil gas ingress

### Energy Efficiency (2026)
- Stricter **Tiered energy performance standards** mandated province-wide
- Example: Insulated Concrete Form (ICF) homes inherently meet Tier 3/4 requirements

## Partial Occupancy Permit (Conditional Permit)

Allows legal occupancy of an **unfinished building** prior to full completion to mitigate unreasonable delays.

### Requirements
- Written authorization from the **Chief Building Official (CBO)**
- Floor plan showing exact scope of area to be occupied and scheduled date

### Life-Safety Conditions Before Occupancy
All of the following must be complete before occupancy is granted:

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Structure complete | Building structure and enclosing walls complete to the roof |
| Fire separations, exits, stair enclosures | Must be finished |
| Hazardous areas | Heavily barricaded to prevent public access |
| Life safety systems | Fire alarms, sprinklers, standpipes, emergency lighting fully operational and witnessed/tested by inspector |
| Plumbing and potable water | Tested and disinfected |
| Final general review reports | From architects, structural, mechanical, and electrical engineers — submitted without deficiencies |
