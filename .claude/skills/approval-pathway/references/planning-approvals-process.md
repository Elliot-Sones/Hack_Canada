# Planning Approvals Process — Ontario / Toronto

## Approval Pathway Summary

```
Does the proposal comply with ALL Zoning By-law standards?
├─ YES + no Site Plan required → Building Permit (as-of-right)
├─ YES + Site Plan required → Site Plan Approval → Building Permit
└─ NO →
     ├─ Minor numeric deviation → Committee of Adjustment (Minor Variance)
     ├─ Use or density not permitted → Zoning By-law Amendment (ZBA)
     └─ OP designation conflict → Official Plan Amendment (OPA) + ZBA
```

---

## Minor Variance — Committee of Adjustment

### What It Is
Relief from a Zoning By-law when a proposed use or structure does not conform exactly to zoning standards (setbacks, lot coverage, height, etc.). The Committee of Adjustment (CoA) has authority under **Planning Act s. 45**.

### Four Statutory Tests (All Four Must Be Met)
1. Is the variance **minor in nature**?
2. Is it **desirable for the appropriate development** or use of the land?
3. Does it maintain the **general intent and purpose of the Zoning By-law**?
4. Does it maintain the **general intent and purpose of the Official Plan**?

A refusal on any single test is sufficient to refuse the entire application.

### Application Process and Timeline

| Step | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Pre-consultation | Meet with municipal planning staff to assess merits and identify required materials |
| Submission | Complete application, property sketches, non-refundable fees |
| Circulation + Notice | Notice mailed to owners within **60 metres**; notice sign posted on property; minimum **10 days** before hearing |
| Hearing and Decision | Staff reports, agency comments, applicant and public representations; decision generally within **30 days** of complete application |
| Notice of Decision | Issued within **10 days** of hearing |
| Appeal Period | **20-day** appeal period from Notice of Decision |
| Decision Final | If no appeals filed, decision becomes final and binding |

### Appeals
- Appeals filed to the **Ontario Land Tribunal (OLT)**
- In Toronto: The **Toronto Local Appeal Body (TLAB)** hears minor variance appeals instead of the OLT

**Important — Bill 23 change**: Third-party neighbours can **no longer appeal** CoA approvals to TLAB as of right (as of Bill 23, 2022). Only the applicant and the municipality can appeal.

## Zoning By-law Amendment (Rezoning)

Required when a proposed development requires a **change in land use** or significant deviations from zoning provisions that cannot be considered "minor."

### Key Facts
- Municipal Council has authority to pass or refuse ZBAs
- Process involves: pre-application consultation → application → circulation → public meeting → staff report → Council decision
- Mandatory decision timelines under **Bill 109**: if Council fails to decide within the legislated period, a deemed refusal occurs (applicant can appeal to OLT)

**Pipeline note**: Always search the AIC Development Applications for active ZBAs on or near the subject parcel before analysis — an in-progress ZBA means base zone standards may change.

---

## Official Plan Amendment (OPA)

Required when a development proposal **conflicts with the Official Plan** designation.

### Key Facts
- Local municipality reviews the OPA
- **Final approval authority** is often the upper-tier municipality (region/county) or the provincial **Ministry of Municipal Affairs and Housing (MMAH)**
- When MMAH is approval authority: province uses a **"One Window Planning Service"** to review alignment with provincial policies
- OPA applications commonly bundled with ZBAs — an "OPA + ZBA" application

## Site Plan Control

An optional tool allowing municipalities to regulate the design and layout of a property (parking, walkways, drainage, landscaping, building-street interface).

### When It Applies
- Commercial, industrial, and multi-unit residential developments (typically **10+ units**)
- **Post-Bill 185 (2024)**: Most residential buildings of **10 units or fewer are EXEMPT** unless within:
  - 300 metres of a railway corridor
  - 120 metres of a wetland
  - Other prescribed areas

### Process
1. Pre-consultation with municipal staff
2. Submit detailed engineering and site plan drawings
3. Technical review by multiple municipal departments
4. Revisions based on staff comments
5. Formal **Site Plan Agreement** registered on title
6. Building permit issued only after Site Plan Agreement registered

## Bill 60 — Fighting Delays, Building Faster Act (2025)

Bill 60 introduces major reforms to planning and approval processes to accelerate housing construction.

### Key Changes

#### As-of-Right Variances (Bypassing CoA)
- Province expanding **"as-of-right" variances** for urban residential land
- Landowners can unilaterally alter prescribed zoning standards (setbacks, etc.) by a "prescribed percentage" **without a minor variance application**
- Reduces burden on Committees of Adjustment for standard residential deviations

#### Site Plan Control Lapsing
- Municipalities can now impose **"lapsing provisions"** on site plan approvals
- If a building permit is not issued before the lapsing period ends → site plan approval **expires**
- Promotes discipline on stalled approvals

#### "Use It or Lose It" Servicing Capacity
- Municipalities can pass by-laws to **reallocate water/wastewater servicing capacity** from inactive/stalled projects to shovel-ready developments
- Draft plans of subdivision and condominium approvals: mandatory **lapsing condition of at least 3 years**

#### Expanded Ministerial Authority and MZOs
- Minister of Municipal Affairs and Housing can now make planning decisions **not required to be consistent with provincial policy statements** (except within Greenbelt)
- For **Ministerial Zoning Orders (MZOs)**: Minister can set strict timelines for finalizing development agreements; can deem conditions satisfied or refer disputes to OLT to prevent delays

#### PMTSA Amendments Streamlined
- Ministerial approval **no longer required** when amending authorized land uses within Protected Major Transit Station Areas (PMTSAs)
- Final approval power transferred to local municipalities, provided residential uses remain authorized

## Timeline Estimates (Approximate, Toronto Context)

| Pathway | Optimistic | Likely | Conservative |
|---------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| As-of-right building permit (residential) | 2–4 weeks | 4–8 weeks | 8–12 weeks |
| Minor variance (no appeal) | 2–3 months | 3–5 months | 5–7 months |
| Minor variance (appealed to TLAB) | 6–9 months | 9–15 months | 15–24 months |
| Zoning By-law Amendment | 12–18 months | 18–30 months | 30–48 months |
| OPA + ZBA | 18–24 months | 30–48 months | 48+ months |

**Note**: Timelines are from application submission. Pre-consultation and drawing preparation add time before the clock starts. OLT appeals add unpredictable delay.
